What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a synthetic analog of human glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), a hormone that plays a crucial role in glucose metabolism and appetite regulation. As a GLP-1 receptor agonist, Semaglutide mimics the action of natural GLP-1, stimulating insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner, suppressing glucagon release, and slowing gastric emptying.

The peptide has been extensively studied for its effects on metabolic health, with research focusing on its mechanisms of action in glucose homeostasis, body weight regulation, and cardiovascular outcomes. Its long half-life (approximately one week) makes it particularly suitable for research requiring sustained GLP-1 receptor activation.
