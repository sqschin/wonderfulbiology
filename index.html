Research Applications

Why SLU-PP-332?

Developed at Saint Louis University, SLU-PP-332 is one of the most studied exercise-mimetic compounds, with growing interest across metabolic research and performance science.

🏃

Exercise Mimetic

Activates the same transcriptional programs induced by endurance exercise — without physical activity. Studied for sedentary metabolic improvement models.

🔥

Fat Oxidation

Promotes fatty acid oxidation and energy expenditure in preclinical models, making it a key compound in obesity and metabolic syndrome research.

⚡

Mitochondrial Biogenesis

Stimulates mitochondrial function and cellular respiration, enhancing energy production at the cellular level via ERRα/β/γ activation.

💪

Muscle Performance

Shown to improve endurance capacity and muscle fiber utilization in animal studies — relevant to muscle wasting disease research.

🧬

Pan-ERR Agonism

Simultaneously activates ERRα (EC₅₀ 98 nM), ERRβ (230 nM), and ERRγ (430 nM) — unlike selective agonists, providing broad metabolic coverage.

🌡️

Metabolic Disease Research

A versatile tool compound for investigating type 2 diabetes, obesity, cardiovascular health, and age-related metabolic decline.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

  • 1

    Binds ERR Receptors (α, β, γ)

    Acts as a pan-agonist across all three estrogen-related receptor isoforms, triggering master metabolic regulators simultaneously.

  • 2

    Activates Exercise Gene Programs

    Upregulates PGC-1α co-activators and downstream genes involved in mitochondrial biogenesis and fatty acid oxidation.

  • 3

    Enhances Cellular Energy Metabolism

    Increases oxygen consumption, OXPHOS gene expression, and metabolic flexibility at the cellular level.

  • 4

    Systemic Metabolic Effects

    In vivo studies show increased whole-body energy expenditure and improved exercise performance metrics.
